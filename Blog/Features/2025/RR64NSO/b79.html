Ridge Racer 64 Joins NSO!

Namco on Nintendo

Today, Nintendo of America has announced that Namco's N64 Ridge Racer 64 is now available to play on Nintendo Switch Online for members with the N64 Expansion Pack. This is the latest offering for the service, coming approximately three months after the last update, which contained Turok and Shadowman.

This marks the 40th N64 game to be released by Nintendo, through their drip feed model, leaving everyone with absolutely no clue what might be next or when.

RR64 Ridge Racer 64 debuted on Nintendo's 5th generation console in February 2000, and garnered review scores of around 85% at the time. Critics praised the polished graphics and N64-exclusive gameplay mechanics but punished the track selection, calling out the sparse three main tracks featured in the title, which contain three variations each.

Featuring 25 cars (21 to unlock), 3 tracks with 3 variations each (including one track exclusive to RR64), online support, and a four-player split-screen mode, RR64 is bound to be an arcade-racing blast, 360° power sliding around those corners at blistering speeds.

The game had notably slick graphics and frame rates back in Y2K, even without using the additional 4 MB Expansion Pak. Namco managed to cram a visually stunning game into the single-player, but had to slightly optimise it for multiplayer, by removing SFX and trimming the detail to keep it from chugging.

The presentation of this version looks superb, showcasing the 16:9 widescreen and 4:3 display modes, as well as footage of the multiplayer modes blazing along at a silky 60 FPS.
