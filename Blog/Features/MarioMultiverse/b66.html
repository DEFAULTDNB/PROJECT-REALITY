Mario in the Multiverse Released!

It's a Meme, Mario! ~ Dec 24, 2024
Mario in the Multiverse

Multiversal Appeal

Today, on Christmas Eve, Rovertronic, aka Rovert, has released their incredible-looking Super Mario 64 ROM Hack collaboration project, Mario in the Multiverse!

The total conversion mod comes with a plethora of new levels, NPCs, and costumes for Mario to wear throughout his jaunt across multiple universes. Notably, the mod features references from various big-name franchises, including Banjo-Kazooie, Zelda, Sonic, Kirby, Final Fantasy 7, Rayman, and even Crash Bandicoot.

Sadly, Mario in the Multiverse is not designed for use on consoles. This is due to the fact that it has been specially crafted with a lot of custom objects and textures that would need extensive optimisation to work perfectly on the N64 console. Should you attempt to play the game on a flashcart, you'll be in for a very rough time, according to Rovertronic himself.

The gameplay is described as Metroidvania style, with 123 stars to collect and a fully kitted-out item store to spend your coins in!

The project notably utilises a fork of UltraSM64, which contains a full decompilation of Super Mario 64 (J), (U), (E), and (SH). This project in particular allows for extra features such as Shindou Rumble Pak support for all regions, saving to 32kbyte/256kbit SRAM, better compression, and includes Wiseguy's instant input patch, allowing compatibility with N64 OS library v2.0L. It also has a work-in-progress implementation of HVQM FMV support.

In order to patch the game to work on your preferred emulator (https://parallel-launcher.ca/ for the best experience), you must obtain the Super Mario 64 (U) (V1.0) [!].z64 ROM, with an MD5 of 20B854B239203BAF6C961B850A4A51A2. Then grab the *.BPS patch and head over to Marc Robledo's fantastic RomPatch.js site to fuse the two together. The links for all of these (except the SM64 ROM) are found below.
