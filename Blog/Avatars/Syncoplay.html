ReCollect64 Chats No. 23

Syncoplay ~ Jan 14, 2025
@Syncoplay

Welcome to ReCollect64 chat Syncoplay! Let's kick-off this Q&A by describing your presence on social media for people:

Syncoplay started when I was building small indie mobile games, inspired by the retro games I grew up with. When I decided to start selling a display stand that I designed for my game room, I repurposed Syncoplay into my retro game company. I have also run a YouTube channel, and am currently writing a book, under that name. Although at this point I mostly just post about obscure Famicom games :)

When did you get into gaming, any formative recollections?

My first console was an Atari 5200 with three games: Pac-Man, Ms. Pac-Man, and Super Breakout. When I received my NES for Christmas at five years old, it changed my world - I would design levels for my pretend Mario-like game on graph paper and insist that others "play through them" with their finger. I was absolutely hooked on everything about games, which led me to follow the career path of game designer into college.

What does your retro gaming collection look like?

Nearly 3000 physical cartridges and discs, displayed in a game room that my wife likes to call "my sculpture" based on how much time I've spent trying to perfect it :) But I don't do sealed/glass case collecting - my games are meant to be played!

Do you have a favourite console?

Famicom is my favorite console, to the point where I decided to write a book about it. PlayStation 2 and Nintendo Switch round out the top three.

What games do you play regularly?

Since I've had kids, it has been much easier to play retro games than modern ones, given the limited time I have to play each week. I love to sift through old retro 8/16 bit stuff to find the weird stuff nobody is talking about. However, I have played through every From Software Souls-like since Demon's Souls (and King's Field before it), and I'm currently playing Metaphor: ReFantazio.

What snacks/drinks keep you both fuelled up for gaming?

Bhuja snack mix, if I can get it. Stuff's amazing. I have an unhealthy Mountain Dew habit but I recently switched to Liquid Death sparkling water to try and stop drinking so much soda. Salsa Verde Doritos also.

Do you have a prized gaming possession?

My gold Punch-Out!! cart for Famicom, Summer Carnival '92 Recca, and Rule of Rose for PS2. Also, one of the only remaining discs for Narbacular Drop - the indie predecessor to Portal.

Do you have any gaming unicorns left to obtain?

I'm currently going for a complete collection of loose Famicom carts, I'm down to less than 50 left to obtain. Moon Crystal and Gimmick are going to be hard to get for a reasonable price though, and there's some really obscure Go/Shogi stuff that I still need to find

Are you looking forward to any games in particular, regarding the recent N64 Recomp projects?

The idea of N64 recomp is interesting, but to be honest I'm more of an original hardware / FPGA player. I rarely, if ever, play games on my PC so I haven't experimented much with the PC ports.

You’re ordering pizza, you get three toppings, what are your combos?

Chicken, Bacon, and a White Sauce/Hot Sauce mix. Add Feta if I get a fourth option.

What’s your most expensive/thrifty retro purchase to date?

My most valuable game is probably Rule of Rose, but I got it through a trade. The most I've ever spent on a single game is likely my copy of Summer Carnival '92 Recca for Famicom. I try to avoid the really high-value consoles like Neo Geo or Saturn because I already spent too much on games :)

And Finally: How many consoles do you own?

You might need a firm number, but I'm not sure I can provide one. I have... a lot. I'll do the best I can.

Original Hardware: 29 + about 15 duplicates

FPGA/Clone Consoles: 8

Arcade (MAME): 1

PC

So... roughly 39 if you don't count duplicates, over 50 if you do. (54)

Duplicates definitely count! Thank you so much for your time and cheers for being part of ReCollect64 Q&A chats!
