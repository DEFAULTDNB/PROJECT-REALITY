Superman 64 VR

Rings of Fire

Those of you who have had the displeasure of playing Superman 64 The New Superman Adventures on the Nintendo 64 will no doubt agree that it is one of, if not the worst games available on the system. Why then, would someone have the impetus to play it nowadays? What if I told you that you could don the cape and play the 64-bit aesthetic recreation of the game through a first-person perspective in virtual reality!

As insane as this all sounds, two years ago (2022) simulation artist Peter Stolmeier, also known as Wackomedia, went to the trouble of remaking the mazes and traps over on itch.io, proving that quite literally everything is better in VR!

Super Sideloading

The game for standalone VR headsets, which is available via the itch.io link below, is 480MB in size, which is around 475MB bigger than the N64 ROM file, and contains the entire Metropolis map, lovingly recreated, from scratch, by Wackomedia.

The game features the main mode and a practice session, where you can fly to your heart's content. The author also came up with an amusing little, copyright-dodging, storyline to accompany the experience:

 

The villainous Luther Lexington has kidnapped your friends Luis Street, Jamie Alyson, and Professor NoJoan Carez and sent them to a virtual world.

It's up to you, Super Human Six, to jack in and save them.

Playing the games as Super Human 6 instead of the titular Man of Steel, you get to begin by jumping into the Stargate-like portal, just like you did on the N64! The actual game itself has a massively better draw distance, far less fog, the timer handily available on your left wrist, your compass on the right, and the colours and textures are far more high-resolution, brighter, and more vibrant. You can still land and jankily launch cars, rescuing innocent civilians and preventing their untimely deaths.

Having given this a go by sideloading the content on my Quest 3, I have to say its quite playable. The sensation of flying is far better portrayed in VR than it ever could have been on the N64, and the game is actually quite enjoyable.

Unfortunately, the entire game is merely minutes long, taking far less than half an hour to complete fully, but Wackomedia saw fit to put the entire credits list for the original game in his version, which I commend thoroughly.
